This is an experiment in illustrating the variance and mean of a set of points in the 2D plane. It is possible to add points to a set of points and the mean and variance is updated and overlayed the data. In order to perform the experiments some statistics and matrix computations will be touched upon.

The Normal Distribution

The normal distribution in one dimension is described by the probability distribution $$ f(x) = \frac{1}{\sigma \sqrt{2\pi}} e^{-\frac{(x-\mu)^2}{2\sigma^2}} $$ where \(\mu\) is the mean and \(\sigma\) is the standard deviation. While the mean is easily understood, the standard deviation measures how spread the distribution is. A large \(\sigma\) implies that numbers further from the mean are more likely.
